Compressing Pooled Datasets

Updated: 11 May 2014

There is a belief that by storing data as compressed SAS datasets then you can get shorter elapsed times since the data can be read in quicker. This is logical. Unfortunately, the SAS language is horrendously inefficient at reading and writing its own compressed datasets so this is actually a huge disadvantage in speed. Never use compressed datasets. Don't store them as compressed. Don't read them in as compressed. The only time it makes sense to use them is in transferring files across to other systems but then zipping them will turn them into smaller files than compressed sas datasets.
